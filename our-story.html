Our Story

Preserving the timeless art of handloom weaving, one saree at a time. We bridge the gap between ancient craftsmanship and modern elegance.

Home / Our Story

"We believe that every saree tells a story ‚Äî of the weaver's patience, the dyer's precision, and the wearer's grace."

Andham began with a simple mission: to bring authentic, handwoven sarees from the skilled artisans of South India to the modern woman who appreciates craftsmanship. What started as a small collection of 20 sarees has grown into a curated destination for those who seek quality over quantity.

Our Journey

From a small weaving cluster to your wardrobe

2018

The Beginning

Founded in a small village near Kanchipuram, Andham started with a single weaving family. We began with 20 Kanjeevaram sarees, sold directly to customers who valued authenticity over mass production.

2019

Expanding the Family

We partnered with 15 more weaving families across Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh, introducing Soft Silk and Gadwal collections while ensuring fair wages and ethical working conditions.

2021

Digital Presence

Launched our online store to reach saree lovers across India and abroad. We invested in photography and storytelling to help customers understand the value of handloom.

2023

Sustainability Focus

Introduced our "Weaver Direct" initiative, ensuring 70% of the price goes directly to artisans. We also launched our eco-friendly packaging and natural dye collections.

2026

Today & Beyond

Now working with 200+ artisan families, we continue to preserve traditional techniques while innovating for contemporary tastes. Every saree still carries the weaver's signature.

The Art of Weaving

Meticulous Craftsmanship

Each Andham saree represents anywhere from 15 days to 6 months of dedicated work. Our weavers use techniques passed down through generations, often using handlooms that are over 50 years old.

The process begins with selecting the finest silk threads, which are then dyed using traditional methods. The warp and weft are carefully set up on the loom, and the magic begins. A single Kanjeevaram saree can contain up to 2,000 threads, each placed by hand.

We don't just sell sarees; we preserve stories, support families, and keep alive an art form that is slowly disappearing in the age of machines.
